📚 §128 — Books of Account (BOA) to be Kept by Company §128 ⭐ IMPORTANT
Company shall prepare BOA, other relevant books & papers and Financial Statements (FS) for every FY.
BOA includes records for:
  • Money received & expended & matters thereof
  • Sales & purchases of goods & services
  • Assets & liabilities of company
  • Items of cost u/s 148 (Cost Audit)
Books & Papers include:
  • BOA, deed, voucher, writings, documents, minutes & registers
Financial Statement includes:
  • Balance sheet at end of FY
  • P&L account [If not for profit company: Income & expenditure account]
  • Cash flow statement [except private company (if startup & has not committed default u/s 92 & 137), OPC, small company & dormant company]
  • Statement of changes in equity
  • Explanatory notes annexed to above documents
FY means period ending on 31st March of the year [If company is incorporated on or after 1st January of a year, then period ending 31st March of next year]
Note: CG may allow any period as FY to company/body corporate (which is holding, subsidiary or associate of company incorporated outside India) & is required to follow different FY for consolidation of its accounts outside India.
Such BOA shall:
  • Give a true & fair view of state of affairs of company & branch office
  • Explain transactions effected at registered office & branches
  • Be kept on accrual basis and as per double entry system of accounting
🏢 Maintenance of Records — Physical vs Electronic Mode
Physical Mode:
  • Company shall prepare & keep BOA at: Registered office of company, OR
  • Other place in India as BOD decides & a notice shall be filed with ROC in Form AOC-5 within 7 days of such decision along with full address of such place
  • For branch office (in or outside India) — deemed compliance if: Proper BOA of branch office is kept at that office, AND Proper summarised returns are sent by branch to company at registered office or other place on periodical basis [If BOA are maintained outside India → quarterly intervals]
Electronic Mode:
  • BOA and books & papers in electronic mode shall remain accessible in India at all times
  • For FY commencing on or after 1st April, 2023 → company (using accounting software) shall use software which has features of recording audit trail of every transaction, creating edit log of changes made in BOA (with date of change) & ensure that audit trail cannot be disabled
  • Information shall be in legible form
  • Proper system (as deem appropriate to audit committee/BOD) for storage, retrieval, display or printout of records. Records shall not be disposed or rendered unusable unless permitted by law
  • Backup of BOA maintained in electronic mode (even if outside India) shall be kept in servers physically located in India on a daily basis
  • Company shall intimate ROC (at time of filing FS) on annual basis: Name, IP address & Location of service provider; If outside India → name & address of person in control of records in India; If on cloud → address as provided by service provider
🧠 Electronic Mode Trick
ALERT — Accessible · Legible · Edit log (audit trail) · Retrieval system · Timely backup (India servers daily)
🔍 Director Inspection, Period for Preservation & Punishment
Director can HIMSELF inspect BOA (NOT through power of attorney/agent/representative) at:
  • India: Can inspect during business hours
  • Outside India: Request company with details of financial information & period of information sought → company shall produce within 15 days of request
📌 (i) Officers & employees of company shall give assistance to directors while making inspection.
(ii) BOA of subsidiary can be inspected only if authorised by BR of holding company.
Period for Preservation of Books:
BOA (along with relevant vouchers) shall be preserved by company for not less than 8 years immediately preceding relevant FY except if:
  • Company incorporated less than 8 years before FY → BOA preserved for entire period before FY
  • Investigation is ordered on company → CG may direct BOA to be kept for more than 8 years
Responsibility for Compliance & Punishment for Default:
Managing Director (MD), Whole Time Director (WTD) in charge of finance, Chief Financial Officer (CFO) or person charged by BOD shall be responsible for compliance u/s 128.
In case of contravention → above mentioned person shall be punishable with fine not less than ₹50,000 which is extendable upto ₹5 lakhs.
📌 Example
B Ltd., having registered office in a remote area, wishes to maintain its BOA at its Mumbai office for convenience. BOD may do so by informing ROC within 7 days of such decision in Form AOC-5.
📊 §129 — Financial Statement (FS) & Consolidated FS (CFS) §129 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
Financial Statement (FS):
  • FS shall give true & fair view of state of affairs of company
  • Comply with Accounting Standards (AS) u/s 133 & be in form provided in Schedule III
  • Not applicable to: Insurance company, Banking company, Company engaged in generation/supply of electricity or company for whom format of FS is specified in act governing it
  • Company shall attach statement (Form AOC-1) containing salient features of FS of company's subsidiary, associate company & JV along with its FS
  • BOD shall lay FS of FY at its AGM
  • If FS do not comply with AS → it shall disclose deviation from AS, reasons for deviation & financial effects from such deviation
Consolidated Financial Statement (CFS):
  • If company has subsidiary or associate company → shall prepare CFS (in same manner as its own) & be laid before AGM
  • CFS is prepared as per Schedule III & applicable AS
  • CFS is NOT required if company meets ALL following conditions [Exemption from CFS]:
    • It is wholly or partially owned subsidiary & all its members other than holding company are intimated (proof of delivery available) & they do not object to not presenting CFS
    • Securities of company are not listed & are not in process of listing
    • Ultimate or intermediate holding company files CFS with ROC as per applicable AS
  • Preparation, adoption & audit provisions of FS shall mutatis mutandis apply to CFS
⚠️ Schedule III: Divided into 2 divisions: Division I (For FS of companies required to comply with AS) and Division II (For FS of companies required to comply with Ind AS).
✍️ §134(1),(6)&(7) — Authentication of FS, CFS and Board's Report
Authentication of FS & CFS:
FS & CFS shall be approved by BOD before they are signed (for submission to auditor) on BOD's behalf by:
  • Chairperson of company (if authorised by BOD), OR
  • 2 directors (1 shall be MD), and
  • Chief Executive Officer (CEO), CFO and CS [if appointed by company]
  • Note: In case of OPC → FS shall be approved only by 1 director
Signed copy of FS & CFS shall be issued, circulated & published along with copy of notes to FS, auditor's report and board's report (BOD Report)
Authentication of Board's Report:
BOD report & annexures shall be signed by:
  • Chairperson of company (if authorised by BOD), OR
  • If not authorised by BOD → atleast 2 directors (1 shall be MD)
  • Note: In case of OPC having 1 director → signed by such director
Type of Board's Report:
  • OPC & Small Company: Abridged Board's Report
  • Otherwise: Board's Report
Punishment for default: Company: Penalty of ₹3 lakhs; Officer in default: Penalty of ₹50,000
📌 Example
A Ltd. has 4 directors (W, X, Y, Z), a MD (Mr. D), and a CS (Mr. A). Hence, FS and BOD Report must be signed by MD, 1 director and CS. If MD or CS does not sign, authentication shall not be valid.

🔄 §130 — Re-opening of Accounts on Court's or Tribunal's Orders §130 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
🚨 Company shall NOT re-open BOA & recast FS on its own!
Company shall NOT re-open BOA & recast FS unless application is made by CG, Income tax authority, SEBI, statutory regulatory body or any concerned person and order is made by court or NCLT that:
Grounds for Re-opening:
  • Such earlier accounts were prepared in fraudulent manner, OR
  • Affairs of company were mismanaged (creating doubt on reliability of FS)
Limit for Re-opening:
Order for reopening of BOA can only relate to 8 FYs preceding current FY.
[But if CG issued direction to keep BOA for longer than 8 years, BOA of such longer period can be re-opened]
📌 Court/NCLT shall give notice to CG, Income tax authority, SEBI, statutory regulatory body & concerned person and take representation made by them into consideration before passing order. Such revised or re-casted accounts shall be final.
📌 Example
In 2024-25, SEBI filed application with NCLT to reopen C Ltd.'s accounts of FY 2012-13, but since it falls beyond 8 years, NCLT cannot allow such reopening (even if fraud or mismanagement is suspected).
🧠 Key Rule
Max 8 FYs before current FY can be re-opened | Court/NCLT order mandatory | Revised accounts = FINAL
✏️ §131 — Voluntary Revision of Financial Statements or Board's Report §131 ⭐ IMPORTANT
If it appears to directors that FS or BOD report of any of preceding 3 FYs does not comply with section 129 or 134 respectively:
Step 1 — BOD Decision & Application
Company may voluntarily make application to NCLT in Form NCLT-1 within 14 days of decision by BOD to prepare revised FS or BOD Report of any of preceding 3 FYs
Step 2 — Advertisement
Company shall advertise such application atleast 14 days before NCLT hearing. Detailed reasons for revision shall be disclosed in BOD report of FY in which revision is made
Step 3 — NCLT Consideration
NCLT shall take into consideration representations made by CG, Income tax authority & auditor before passing any order
Step 4 — NCLT Approval & ROC Filing
If approved, certified copy of NCLT order shall be filed with ROC within 30 days of its receipt
Step 5 — GM & Approval
General meeting may be called & notice published in English & vernacular language newspaper. Revised FS and statement of directors & auditors shall be approved in GM
Step 6 — Final Filing
Revised FS (along with auditor statement) or BOD report shall be filed with ROC within 30 days of approval
🚨 Such revised FS or BOD report shall NOT be prepared or filed more than once in a FY
If previous FS/BOD report are sent to members, ROC or laid in AGM, revision shall be confined to:
  • Correction of such part of FS or BOD report which do NOT comply with section 129 or 134
  • Making necessary consequential alteration
CG may make rules for: Presentation of FS or BOD report; Functions of auditor in case of revised FS or BOD report; Steps required to be taken by directors.
🧠 Key Numbers
Preceding 3 FYs | NCLT-1 within 14 days of BOD | Advertise 14 days before NCLT | ROC filing 30 days | Cannot be done more than ONCE per FY
🏛️ §132 — National Financial Reporting Authority (NFRA) §132 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
CG may constitute NFRA to provide for matters relating to accounting & auditing standards.
  • Companies whose securities are listed in or outside India
  • Unlisted public company having (as at end of preceding FY): Paid-up capital ≥ ₹500 crores, OR Turnover ≥ ₹1,000 crores, OR Outstanding loans, debentures & deposits ≥ ₹500 crores
  • Insurance company, banking company, electricity company or special act company
  • Person (on reference made by CG in public interest)
  • Body corporate incorporated outside India (which is subsidiary/associate of Indian company on which NFRA is applicable) whose income or net worth > 20% of consolidated income or net worth of company
Note: NFRA shall continue to be applicable to company or body corporate for 3 years after it ceases to fall in applicability criteria.
  • CG shall appoint Chairperson & other members ≤ 15 (part-time or full-time)
  • Chairperson & full-time members shall NOT be associated with any audit firm (during appointment & 2 years after ceasing to hold appointment)
  • Chairperson & members shall make declaration to CG for no conflict of interest
  • Executive body = chairperson & full-time members — responsible for efficient discharge of functions
Part-time members of NFRA:
  • Ex-officio member of MCA (not below rank of Joint Secretary)
  • Ex-officio CAG member (not below Accountant General or Principal Director rank)
  • Ex-officio member of RBI and SEBI (not below rank of Executive Director)
  • President of ICAI (ex-officio)
  • Chairperson of Accounting Standards Board, ICAI (ex-officio)
  • Chairperson of Auditing & Assurance Standards Board, ICAI (ex-officio)
  • 2 experts from field of accountancy, auditing, finance or law
  • NFRA protects interests of public, investors, creditors & others by establishing accounting & auditing standards and effective oversight
  • Maintain details of auditors in company & body corporate governed by NFRA
  • Recommend AS and auditing standards (SA) for approval by CG
  • Monitor & enforce compliance with AS & SA
  • Promote awareness of compliance of AS and SA
  • Oversee quality of service of professionals for compliance with standards
  • Co-operate with national & international organisation of independent audit regulators to establish & oversee adherence to AS and SA
  • Perform such other functions & duties as necessary
Powers vested in Civil Court:
  • Discovery & production of BOA & documents (at place & time specified by NFRA)
  • Summoning & enforcing attendance of persons & examining them on oath
  • Inspection of books, registers and documents of persons summoned
  • Issuing commissions for examination of witnesses or documents
Note: No institute or body shall initiate or continue proceedings for matters where NFRA has initiated investigation.
If Misconduct is Proved, NFRA may order:
  • Imposing penalty:
    • Individual: Penalty between ₹1 lakh and 5 times of fees received
    • Firms: Penalty between ₹5 lakh and 10 times of fees received
  • Debarring member or firm to be appointed as auditor, internal auditor or performing valuation for 6 months to 10 years
Person aggrieved by NFRA order may appeal to Appellate Tribunal.
Other Provisions of NFRA:
  • NFRA shall maintain books in manner as CG prescribe (in consultation with CAG)
  • Accounts shall be audited by CAG at such intervals as specified by him
  • Accounts along with audit report shall be forwarded annually by NFRA to CG
  • NFRA shall prepare annual report of activities during the FY & forward it to CG who shall lay annual report & audit report before each House of Parliament
Punishment for Default: If company, officer, auditor or any person contravenes NFRA Rules → punishable u/s 450.
📐 §133 — Accounting Standards (AS) §133
CG may prescribe Accounting Standards (AS) recommended by ICAI in consultation with & after examination of recommendation by National Financial Reporting Authority (NFRA).
📌 Until NFRA was constituted, CG may prescribe AS as recommended by ICAI in consultation with & after examination of recommendation by National Advisory Committee on Accounting Standards (NACAS).
Schedule III — Two Divisions:
  • Division I: For FS of companies required to comply with AS
  • Division II: For FS of companies required to comply with Ind AS
📋 §134(3),(3A),(4)&(5) — Content of Board's Report §134 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
BOD Report is prepared as per standalone FS and reports performance of subsidiary, associate & JV and it is annexed to FS of company.
Content to be included in BOD Report and Abridged BOD Report:
  • Web address where annual return is placed
  • Number of BOD meetings
  • Directors' Responsibility Statement
  • Directors appointed or resigned during year
  • State of company's affairs
  • Financial summary or highlights
  • Material change affecting financial position of company (due to event that occurred between end of FY to which FS relate and date of report)
  • Frauds reported by auditors (except those reportable to CG)
  • Explanation/comment by BOD on qualification, reservation, adverse remark or disclaimer by auditor in his report or by CS in secretarial audit report
  • Significant orders passed by regulators, courts or NCLT impacting going concern
  • Related Party Transactions
Additional Content to be included in BOD Report:
  • Statement indicating manner of evaluation of performance of BOD & committee [For listed company or public company having paid-up capital of ≥ ₹25 crores as at end of preceding FY]
    Exception: Government company which has not committed default u/s 92 & 137
  • Statement of compliance with Internal Complaints Committee under Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition & Redressal) Act
  • Amount BOD recommends as dividend
  • Other matters prescribed under Companies Act, 2013 and Rule 8 of Companies (Accounts) Rules, 2014 such as: change in nature of business, details of deposits, applicability of cost records, companies which became/ceased as subsidiary/associate/JV during year, etc.
  • Directors had prepared annual accounts on going concern basis
  • Applicable AS are followed for preparation of annual accounts (along with proper explanation of material departures)
  • Directors had selected & applied accounting policies consistently and made estimates reasonable & prudent to give true & fair view of company
  • Directors took proper & sufficient care for maintenance of accounts, safeguarding assets and preventing & detecting fraud and other irregularities
  • For listed company, directors have laid down internal financial controls to be followed and such controls are adequate & operating effectively
  • Directors had devised proper systems to ensure compliance with applicable laws and that such systems were adequate & operating effectively
Punishment for default: Company: Penalty of ₹3 lakhs; Officer in default: Penalty of ₹50,000
🌱 §135 — Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) §135 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
Company (including foreign company having branch in India) shall constitute CSR Committee if it meets ANY of the following criteria during immediately preceding FY:
  • Net worth of ₹500 crores or more, OR
  • Turnover of ₹1,000 crores or more, OR
  • Net profit (before tax) of ₹5 crores or more
Net worth = aggregate of paid-up share capital, reserves (created out of profits) & securities premium after reducing accumulated losses, deferred expenditure & miscellaneous expenditure not written off [does NOT include reserves created from revaluation, write-back of depreciation and amalgamation]
Net profit = as per Section 198, but shall NOT include: Profit from overseas branch, and Dividend received from Indian company (on which CSR is applicable)
⚠️ If amount to be spent on CSR is ≤ ₹50 lakhs, CSR Committee is NOT required [and functions of committee shall be discharged by BOD]. Company having amount in Unspent CSR Account shall constitute CSR Committee.
BOD shall ensure that company spends atleast 2% of average net profits of preceding 3 FYs or lesser period (if company has not completed 3 FYs).
SituationTreatment
CSR SurplusNOT treated as profit; shall be used for same project OR transferred (within 6 months from expiry of FY) to Unspent CSR Account or fund specified in Schedule VII
Excess Spend (excess does not include surplus)Can set off excess against immediately succeeding 3 FYs if BOD resolution is passed
Fails to spend — ongoing projectTransfer to Unspent CSR Account within 30 days of end of FY → Spend as per CSR Policy within 3 FYs of transfer → If fails → transfer to fund in Schedule VII within 30 days of completion of 3rd FY
Fails to spend — NOT ongoing projectTransfer within 6 months of expiry of FY to fund specified in Schedule VII
CSR on Capital AssetCSR may be spent for creation/acquisition of capital asset, held by section 8 company, Registered Public Trust or Society (with charitable objects & CSR Registration Number), beneficiaries of CSR project or public authority
Admin ExpenseBOD ensure administrative overhead shall NOT exceed 5% of CSR expense of FY [Note: Does not include expenses incurred directly for designing, implementation, monitoring & evaluation of CSR project]
Penal provision for non-compliance:
Company: Penalty of lower of "twice the amount required to be transferred to fund in Schedule VII/Unspent CSR Account" or "₹1 crore"
Officer in default: Penalty of lower of "1/10th of amount required to be transferred" or "₹2 lakhs"
📌 Example
S Ltd. incorporated on 01.04.19. Net profit before tax for FY 19-20 and 20-21 was ₹5 crore and ₹7 crore. Hence, company must spend 2% of average net profits of the last 2 years (since incorporating for 2 years only), i.e., 2% of ₹6 crores = ₹12 lakhs on CSR activities.
PointDetail
Composition3 or more directors [1 of whom shall be independent director].
Note: If company is not required to appoint independent director → 2 or more directors (without any independent director). Private company having 2 directors → committee shall have such 2 directors. Foreign company → 2 persons [1 authorised to accept notice, other nominated by foreign company]
Duties of CSR CommitteeFormulate & recommend CSR Policy (indicating activities to be undertaken in areas specified in Schedule VII) to BOD; Recommend amount to be incurred on CSR; Monitor CSR policy of company
Annual Action PlanCSR Committee shall recommend annual action plan to BOD including: List of projects in Schedule VII areas; Manner of execution; Utilisation of funds; Monitoring & reporting mechanism; Need & impact assessment details
Note: BOD may alter plan at any time (as per committee's recommendations)
Duty of BODAfter considering recommendations of CSR Committee, BOD shall approve CSR policy, disclose it in BOD report and place it (along with composition of committee & approved project) on company's website
Schedule VII Activities include: Promoting gender equality, sports, employment, health, education; Har Ghar Tiranga Campaign; eradicating hunger, poverty; contribution to university; Swach Bharat Kosh, PM National Relief Fund, PM CARES Fund or fund setup by CG; Slum area development; Rural development; Disaster Management; Benefit of armed force veteran, etc.
CSR shall NOT include:
  • Activity undertaken in pursuance of normal course of business
  • Activity undertaken by company outside India (except training of Indian sports personnel representing State or Union territory at national/international level)
  • Contribution to political party (directly or indirectly)
  • Activities benefitting employees of company
  • Activities supported on sponsorship basis to derive marketing of its products or services
  • Activities for fulfilment of other statutory obligations
Company shall give preference to local area where it operates for spending CSR.
📌 Example
A Ltd. sponsors school fees of employees' children as part of its CSR welfare initiative. But since activity benefits its employees, it does not qualify as CSR.
CSR Implementation [Onus lies on BOD]:
  • By company itself, OR
  • Through Section 8 company or registered public trust or society: Charitable entity established by company (singly or with other company); Established by CG/SG; OR Charitable entity with 3 year experience of undertaking similar activities
  • Through statutory body established by Act of Parliament/State legislature
Such entity shall register with CG by filing Form CSR-1 (verified by CA/CS/CMA) with ROC → CSR registration number is generated on submission.
CSR Reporting:
  • BOD Report of company (required to constitute CSR committee) shall include annual report on CSR
  • Company having average CSR obligation ≥ ₹10 crores in preceding 3 FYs shall undertake impact assessment (through independent agency) of CSR projects having outlays ≥ ₹1 crore & which are completed ≥ 1 year before such assessment
  • Impact assessment report shall be placed before BOD & annexed to annual report on CSR
  • Company undertaking impact assessment may book expenditure towards CSR of FY upto higher of 2% of CSR expenditure of FY or ₹50 lakhs
(i) Company may collaborate with other companies for undertaking CSR activity [provided CSR committee of each company is in position to report separately on projects]
(ii) CSR activities should be undertaken in project or programme mode [One-off events like marathon, charitable contribution, etc. do NOT qualify as CSR]
(iii) Company may engage international organisation only for designing, monitoring & evaluation of CSR projects/programmes as per CSR policy

T · N · P

👥 §136 — Right of Members to Copies of Audited Financial Statement §136
Serving documents to entitled persons:
Copy of FS, CFS, auditor's report and document required to be attached to FS to be laid down at AGM shall be sent atleast 21 days before meeting [14 days in case of Section 8 company] to every member of company, trustee of debenture-holder & all other entitled persons.
Company shall provide copy of separate FS of subsidiary (audited or unaudited) to member who asks for it.
Documents can be sent less than 21 days before meeting if agreed by:
Company having share capital: Majority number of members holding atleast 95% of paid-up share capital (& voting power) of company
Company having no share capital: Members holding atleast 95% voting power at meeting
Listed Company — Deemed compliance if atleast 21 days before meeting:
  • Copy of documents are available for inspection at registered office during working hours, AND
  • Statement of salient features of documents in Form AOC-3 or copy of documents is sent to members & trustees (unless shareholder ask for full FS)
Nidhi Company — Deemed compliance with notice of AGM & above requirements even if notice is not served to members holding shares (individually or jointly) upto lower of face value of ₹1,000 or 1% of paid-up share capital but:
  • Intimation is given by public notice in newspaper (circulated in district where registered office is situated) stating date, time & venue of AGM, AND
  • FS (with enclosure) can be inspected at registered office and are affixed on notice board of company
Manner of sending FS to members of company:
  • Listed Company and Public company having net worth > ₹1 crore and turnover > ₹10 crore → Electronic Mode to members having shares in: Dematerialized format; Not in demat format but consented to receive by electronic mode; Others sent as per section 20
Accounts to be placed on Website:
Listed company shall place its FS & CFS, separate audited accounts of all subsidiary and other documents required to be attached to them on its website.
Inspection: Company shall allow every member or trustee of debenture-holder to inspect above documents at its registered office during business hours.
Punishment for default: Company: Penalty of ₹25,000; Officer in default: Penalty of ₹5,000.

🔍 §138 — Internal Audit §138 ⭐ IMPORTANT
Companies Required to Appoint Internal Auditor:
Company TypeCriteria (during preceding FY)
Listed companyMandatory — all listed companies
Unlisted public companyTurnover ≥ ₹200 crores, OR Outstanding loans/borrowings from banks or PFI exceeding ₹100 crores at any time, OR Paid-up share capital ≥ ₹50 crores, OR Outstanding deposits ≥ ₹25 crores at any time
Private companyTurnover ≥ ₹200 crores, OR Outstanding loans/borrowings from banks or PFI exceeding ₹100 crores at any time
🧠 Thresholds Mnemonic
Unlisted: T200 + L100 + P50 + D25 | Private: T200 + L100
T=Turnover ₹200cr | L=Loans ₹100cr (at ANY TIME) | P=Paid-up capital ₹50cr | D=Deposits ₹25cr (at ANY TIME)
Qualifications of Internal Auditor:
Internal auditor shall either be a CA, CMA or such professional (whether engaged in practice or not) as decided by BOD. Also, Internal auditor may or may not be an employee of company.
Other Provisions:
  • Internal auditor may be either an individual, partnership firm or body corporate
  • Audit Committee or BOD (in consultation with Internal Auditor) shall formulate scope, functioning, periodicity and methodology of conducting internal audit
📌 Example
P Pvt. Ltd. had outstanding loan of ₹75 crores at end of last FY out of total ₹110 crores loan availed during last FY. As it crossed ₹100 crore limit at any time during last FY, it shall appoint internal auditor.

Q1 · 4M · §128 B Ltd. has its registered office in a remote area and wishes to maintain its BOA at its Mumbai office for convenience. Can it do so? What is the procedure? What are the requirements for Electronic Mode of maintaining BOA?
PROVISIONSection 128: Company shall prepare & keep BOA at registered office of company OR at other place in India as BOD decides and a notice shall be filed with ROC in Form AOC-5 within 7 days of such decision along with full address of such place.
APPLICATIONYes, B Ltd. can maintain BOA at its Mumbai office. BOD may do so by passing BOD resolution and informing ROC in Form AOC-5 within 7 days of such decision along with full address of Mumbai office. For Electronic Mode: BOA shall remain accessible in India at all times, information in legible form, audit trail feature enabled from 1 Apr 2023, backup on servers in India on daily basis, intimate ROC annually (name, IP address, location of service provider).
CONCLUSIONYes, it is possible. BOD resolution + Form AOC-5 to ROC within 7 days with full address. Electronic Mode — servers in India, audit trail cannot be disabled, daily backup, legible form, annual intimation to ROC.
Q2 · 4M · §130 In 2024-25, SEBI filed an application with NCLT to reopen C Ltd.'s accounts of FY 2012-13 on grounds of fraud. Can NCLT allow reopening?
PROVISIONSection 130: Order for reopening of BOA can only relate to 8 FYs preceding current FY. [Exception: If CG issued direction to keep BOA for longer than 8 years, BOA of such longer period can be re-opened]
APPLICATIONCurrent FY = 2024-25. 8 years preceding current FY = FY 2016-17 onwards. FY 2012-13 is beyond 8 years from 2024-25. No direction by CG to keep BOA for longer period is mentioned.
CONCLUSIONNCLT cannot allow reopening of FY 2012-13 accounts as it falls beyond the 8-year limit prescribed under §130, even if fraud or mismanagement is suspected.
Q3 · 5M · §135 S Ltd. incorporated on 01.04.2019. Net profit before tax for FY 2019-20 and 2020-21 was ₹5 crore and ₹7 crore respectively. Is S Ltd. required to constitute CSR Committee for FY 2021-22? If yes, how much must it spend on CSR?
PROVISIONSection 135: Company with net profit (before tax) ≥ ₹5 crores in immediately preceding FY is required to constitute CSR Committee and spend atleast 2% of average net profits of preceding 3 FYs or lesser period (if company has not completed 3 FYs).
APPLICATION
FY 2020-21 net profit = ₹7 crores (≥ ₹5 crores threshold). So S Ltd. must constitute CSR Committee for FY 2021-22.
Average net profit (only 2 FYs completed) = (₹5 + ₹7) / 2 = ₹6 crores.
CSR obligation = 2% × ₹6 crores = ₹12 lakhs.
Since CSR amount = ₹12 lakhs > ₹50 lakhs? → NO, ₹12 lakhs < ₹50 lakhs → CSR Committee NOT required — functions discharged by BOD.
CONCLUSIONS Ltd. is required to spend ₹12 lakhs on CSR activities in FY 2021-22. However, since CSR obligation ≤ ₹50 lakhs → CSR Committee is NOT required; BOD shall discharge the functions of CSR Committee.
Q4 · 4M · §137 S Ltd. held its AGM on 30th September 2025 but did not adopt accounts at AGM. Accounts were adopted in adjourned AGM held on 15th November 2025. State the complete filing requirements u/s 137.
PROVISIONSection 137: Unadopted FS shall be filed with ROC within 30 days of AGM and ROC shall record them as provisional. If FS adopted in adjourned AGM → filed within 30 days of adjourned AGM.
APPLICATION
  1. By 30th October 2025 — File unadopted FS with ROC (within 30 days of AGM on 30th September 2025). ROC records as PROVISIONAL.
  2. By 15th December 2025 — After adoption in adjourned AGM (15th November 2025), file adopted FS with ROC (within 30 days of adjourned AGM).
CONCLUSIONS Ltd. must file unadopted FS by 30th October 2025 (provisional), and after adoption, file within 30 days of adjourned AGM i.e. by 15th December 2025. Failure to file unadopted FS within 30 days of original AGM = violation of §137.
Q5 · 4M · §132 What happens if misconduct of a CA firm is proved before NFRA? State the powers of NFRA in investigation and if misconduct is proved.
PROVISIONSection 132: NFRA has powers vested in civil court to investigate. If misconduct is proved, NFRA may impose penalty and debar member/firm from acting as auditor for 6 months to 10 years.
APPLICATION
Powers to Investigate: Discovery & production of BOA; Summoning & examining persons on oath; Inspection of books, registers and documents; Issuing commissions for examination.
If misconduct proved against a CA firm:
• Penalty: between ₹5 lakh and 10 times of fees received
• Debarring firm from being appointed as auditor, internal auditor or performing valuation for 6 months to 10 years
CONCLUSIONNFRA may impose penalty of ₹5L to 10× fees received AND debar the firm from acting as auditor/internal auditor/valuer for 6 months to 10 years. Person aggrieved by NFRA order may appeal to Appellate Tribunal.
Q6 · 4M · §138 P Pvt. Ltd. had outstanding loan of ₹75 crores at end of last FY but availed ₹110 crores total loan from banks during last FY. Is P Pvt. Ltd. required to appoint internal auditor u/s 138?
PROVISIONSection 138: Private company having outstanding loans or borrowings from banks or PFI exceeding ₹100 crores at any time during preceding FY shall appoint internal auditor.
APPLICATIONAlthough outstanding loan at end of last FY = ₹75 crores (below ₹100 crores), P Pvt. Ltd. had availed total ₹110 crores loan during last FY. This means at some point during the FY, outstanding loans exceeded ₹100 crores. The test is "at any time" during preceding FY, not at end of FY. ₹110 crores > ₹100 crores limit = triggered.
CONCLUSIONYes, P Pvt. Ltd. must appoint internal auditor as outstanding loans crossed ₹100 crore limit at some point during last FY (at any time = key condition).

🚫 §141(3) — Disqualifications §141(3) ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
🧠 Master Mnemonic
" C A S H A ko audit nahi karne do! "
C = Body Corporate (LLP excluded from this disq.)
A = Officer / Appointed employee of company (or partner/employee of officer/employee)
S = Security/Interest holder — relative or partner holds security/interest in company, Assoc., Subsidiary, Holding, or Any subsidiary of Holding (CASHA group) with face value > ₹1 lakh
H = Has indebtedness > ₹5 lakhs to CASHA group
A = Audit in more than 20 companies (partner/individual limit)
  • All relatives together may hold security of face value not exceeding ₹1 lakh in such companies
  • If relative acquires security exceeding ₹1 lakh after appointment → auditor must take corrective action within 60 days
  • Guarantor for indebtedness of third person in excess of ₹1 lakh to CASHA → disqualified
  • Business relationship with CASHA = transaction for commercial purpose (except: professional services transactions & transactions in ordinary course at arm's length price)
  • Partner/partner of firm who is auditor in more than 20 companies as on appointment date
RuleDetail
Individual limitMax 20 companies
Firm limitNo. of CA partners × 20
NOT counted in 20 limitPrivate company (paid-up <₹100 cr, not defaulted u/s 92 & 137), Dormant company, OPC, Small company
⚠️ Private co limit: Appointment in private company having paid-up share capital <₹100 crores (not defaulted u/s 92 & 137), dormant company, OPC and small company — NOT be considered within limit of 20 companies.
In case of firm, limit of 20 audits is per person but if a person is partner in multiple firms, all such firms in which he is a partner will together be entitled to 20 audits on his account.
Vacation of Office: If auditor incurs any disqualification AFTER appointment → shall vacate office → treated as casual vacancy
🔢 §141 — Solved Example: 20 Company Limit
Example: ABC & Co. has Mr. A, B and C as partners who are auditors in 4, 6 and 10 companies respectively. What is maximum number of audits?
Solution:
Total firm limit = 3 × 20 = 60
Audits already held = 4 + 6 + 10 = 20
ABC & Co. can accept 40 more audits
Partner A: 20−4 = 16 more | Partner B: 20−6 = 14 more | Partner C: 20−10 = 10 more

📅 Term & Cooling Period §139(2)
Company TypeIndividualFirm / LLP
Listed / Unlisted public (paid-up ≥₹10cr) / Private (paid-up ≥₹50cr) / Public borrowings/deposits ≥₹50cr 1 term of 5 years 2 terms of 5 years each
Other companiesAny number of terms
⏳ Cooling Period = 5 years — after completing term, NOT eligible for re-appointment in SAME company.
Key: Cooling period does NOT apply to subsidiary, holding, or any other company — ONLY same company!
  • On date of appointment of audit firm: shall NOT have any common partner with outgoing firm whose tenure expired in immediately preceding FY. If common partners exist → cooling period of 5 years.
  • If a partner (who certifies FS) retires from audit firm (which has completed term) and joins another firm → such other firm also becomes ineligible for 5 years
  • A person shall NOT be eligible to be appointed as auditor if it is associated with outgoing auditor under same network (same brand name, trade name or common control)
  • Cooling off period for a company does NOT apply to any other company (example: such auditor can audit company's subsidiary or any service except statutory audit)

📌 §177 — Audit Committee: Companies Required to Constitute
Must Constitute Audit Committee:
  • Listed public company
  • Unlisted public co having (on date of last audited FS):
  • Paid up capital ≥ ₹10 crores, OR
  • Turnover ≥ ₹100 crores, OR
  • Aggregate outstanding loan, borrowings, debenture & deposits ≥ ₹50 crores

📖 §2(35) — Definition & Types of Dividend §2(35)
Dividend includes interim dividend.
Dividend is declared as a proportion of Nominal / Face Value of share.
Not paid in Kind — shall be paid in cash, cheque, dividend warrant or any electronic mode only.
🔵 Interim Dividend
  • Declared by BOD
  • During Financial Year (FY), OR
  • Post closure of FY till holding AGM
  • Can be revoked after declaration with consent of all shareholders
🟢 Final Dividend
  • Recommended by BOD & approved by shareholders at AGM
  • Rate of dividend recommended by BOD cannot be increased by shareholders
  • Once declared → becomes liability/debt of company
  • Cannot be revoked in any case
🧠 Key Difference Trick
Interim = BOD declares, CAN revoke (with all shareholders' consent)
Final = AGM approves, CANNOT revoke — becomes DEBT of company
💰 Payment of Dividend §123(5):
  • Persons whose names are in register of members as on record date are entitled to dividend
  • Dividend is payable to registered shareholder or to his order or to his banker
  • Dividend shall be paid in cash, cheque, dividend warrant or any electronic mode [but NOT in kind]
💰 §123(1) & §123(3) — Sources of Dividend §123(1)(3) ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
🔵 Source for Interim Dividend
  • Surplus in P&L account
  • Profits of FY for which dividend is to be declared
  • Profit till quarter preceding declaration of dividend
🟢 Source for Final Dividend
  • Current FY profit after depreciation as per Schedule II
  • Previous FY profit (credit balance in P&L account and free reserves)
  • Both the above profits combined
  • Money provided by CG/SG for payment of dividend by company due to guarantee given by such government
⚠️ Free Reserves: Free reserves are reserves available for distribution as dividend (as per last audited balance sheet). But following are NOT treated as free reserves:
• Unrealised gain, notional gain, revaluation reserve and capital reserve
• Change in carrying amount of asset/liability which is recognised in equity
📌 Before Declaration of Dividend:
Previous Year (PY) losses & depreciation which were not provided in such years are set off against current year profit
Transfer of profit to reserves (before declaration of dividend) is left to the discretion of company. Company may also decide NOT to transfer any amount to reserves.
📉 §123(1) — Declaration When Inadequacy or Absence of Profits §123(1) ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
🚨 Yeh topic ICAI exams mein numerically aata hai — conditions aur limits exactly yaad rakho!
📌 Interim Dividend — Inadequacy of Profits:
If company has incurred loss during current FY upto quarter preceding declaration → dividend shall NOT be declared at rate higher than average rate of dividend declared by company during immediately preceding 3 FYs
[Not applicable if dividend is NOT declared in all 3 preceding FYs]
📌 Final Dividend from Accumulated Profits (Free Reserves) — 5 Conditions:
  1. Dividend shall NOT be declared at rate higher than average rate of dividend declared by company during immediately preceding 3 FYs [Not applicable if dividend is not declared in all 3 preceding FYs]
  2. Amount drawn from accumulated profits shall NOT exceed 10% of paid-up share capital & free reserves (as per last audited FS)
  3. Drawn amount is first utilised to set off losses of FY in which dividend is declared
  4. Balance of reserves (after withdrawal & adjusting loss) shall NOT fall below 15% of paid up share capital (as per last audited FS)
  5. This provision is NOT applicable to 100% government company (i.e., company where entire paid up capital is held by CG, SG, or both)
🧠 5 Conditions Mnemonic — "RADOS"
R · A · D · O · S
Rate — not higher than average of last 3 FYs
Amount — max 10% of (paid-up capital + free reserves)
Draw — first utilise to set off current FY losses
Only — balance of reserves must stay above 15% of paid-up capital
Special — not applicable to 100% Govt company
📊 Solved Example (from ICAI Notes)
Problem: C Ltd. has paid-up capital of ₹200 lakhs & free reserve of ₹240 lakhs. It incurred loss of ₹30 lakhs during FY but wants to declare dividend. In 3 PYs, it declared dividend @ 9%, 10% and 12%. Can it declare dividend from free reserves?

Solution:
➤ Average dividend declared during last 3 FY = (9+10+12)/3 = 10.33% (maximum permissible rate)
➤ Amount up to 10% of (paid-up capital & Reserves) can be withdrawn = 10% × ₹440 lakhs = ₹44 lakhs
   After adjusting ₹30 lakh loss → Only ₹14 lakhs can be withdrawn
➤ Balance reserve = ₹196 lakhs [i.e., after withdrawal & adjustment of loss (₹240 − 30 − 14 lakhs)]
   This is more than 15% of paid-up capital = 15% × ₹200 = ₹30 lakhs ✅

Conclusion: Company can declare dividend upto ₹14 lakh, i.e., 7% of ₹200 lakh paid-up capital
📌 Note: If year to which free reserves relate is given, it is assumed that current year loss (if any) is already adjusted. Otherwise, current year loss has to be adjusted from free reserves.

Q1 · 5M · §123 C Ltd. has paid-up capital of ₹200 lakhs & free reserve of ₹240 lakhs. It incurred loss of ₹30 lakhs during FY but wants to declare dividend. In 3 PYs, it declared dividend @ 9%, 10% and 12%. What is the maximum dividend C Ltd. can declare?
PROVISIONSection 123(1): Company may declare dividend out of accumulated profits transferred to reserves subject to: (a) Rate not higher than average of last 3 FYs, (b) Amount max 10% of paid-up + free reserves, (c) First set off current year losses, (d) Balance reserves must stay ≥ 15% of paid-up capital.
APPLICATION
Step 1: Average dividend = (9+10+12)/3 = 10.33% (max permissible rate)
Step 2: Max withdrawal = 10% × (₹200 + ₹240) = 10% × ₹440 = ₹44 lakhs
Step 3: First set off loss of ₹30 lakhs → Remaining = ₹44 − ₹30 = ₹14 lakhs
Step 4: Balance reserves = ₹240 − ₹30 (loss) − ₹14 (withdrawal) = ₹196 lakhs
   15% of paid-up capital = 15% × ₹200 = ₹30 lakhs. ₹196L > ₹30L ✅
CONCLUSIONC Ltd. can declare dividend of maximum ₹14 lakhs i.e., 7% of ₹200 lakh paid-up capital (not exceeding 10.33% rate).
Q2 · 4M · §127 Mr. X instructed company to pay dividend to his bank account but transaction fails due to incorrect details and company doesn't inform X about it. Is company liable u/s 127?
PROVISIONSection 127: If company fails to pay declared dividend within 30 days → Company: interest @18% p.a. Director: imprisonment upto 2 years + ₹1,000/day fine. Exemptions available for specific situations.
APPLICATIONDividend was not paid due to incorrect bank details — this could be exemption if company had communicated to Mr. X. However, company did NOT inform Mr. X about the failure. Hence, the exemption of "shareholder's directions cannot be complied and same has been communicated to him" does NOT apply. Company has defaulted in its duty to communicate.

APPLICATION

S Ltd.: Dividend withheld due to court order on family dispute = operation of law. This falls under exemption (i) of §127 — "could not be paid by reason of operation of law."
CONCLUSIONNeither H Ltd. nor S Ltd. is liable u/s 127. Both situations fall within specific exemptions provided under the section.

📘 §1 & §4 — Introduction to LLP Act, 2008 §1 & §4
The LLP Act, 2008 is applicable to whole of India.
Note: Indian Partnership Act, 1932 is NOT applicable to LLPs.
What is LLP?
  • LLP is alternative corporate business vehicle that gives benefit of limited liability at low compliance cost
  • Allows flexibility to partners in organising their internal structure as traditional partnership
  • LLP is a suitable vehicle for small enterprises and for investment by venture capital
  • MCA & ROC shall administrate LLP Act
  • CG has authority to frame rules & amend act
Advantages of LLP:
  • Easy to form
  • Partners have limited liability
  • Organised & operates on basis of agreement
  • Provides flexibility (no detailed legal & procedural requirement)
  • Easy to dissolve
Characteristics of LLP:
(i) Body Corporate
(ii) Perpetual Succession
(iii) Separate Legal Entity
(iv) Partners entitled to manage business of LLP
(v) Artificial Legal Person
(vi) Liability of partners is limited to their agreed contribution [LLP liable for full extent of its assets]
(vii) Business for profit only [Cannot be formed for charitable purpose]
(viii) Mutual rights & duties of partners are governed by LLP agreement [In absence of agreement — governed by Schedule I]
(ix) Mutual Agency [No partner is liable for unauthorized actions of other partners]
(x) Documents to be filed shall be authenticated by partner or DP
(xi) CG can investigate into affairs of LLP
(xii) Common Seal [LLP may have common seal (if it decides to have) which shall remain under custody of responsible official & be affixed in presence of atleast 2 DPs]
📌 Body corporate means company under Companies Act or incorporated outside India and LLP under this Act or incorporated outside India [but does NOT include firm, co-operative society, HUF and other body corporate specified by CG].
📌 LLP agreement is an agreement between partners or between LLP & partners determining their mutual rights & duties.
👥 §5, 6 & 22 — Partners of LLP §5,6,22 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
Who can be Partner?
  • Individual & body corporate can be partner in LLP
  • Persons subscribing their names to incorporation document shall be partners of LLP
  • Other persons may become partner as per LLP agreement
  • LLP shall have atleast 2 partners [No limit on maximum partners]
Who CANNOT be Partner?
An individual cannot become partner of LLP if he is:
  • Found to be of unsound mind by competent court
  • Undischarged insolvent
  • Application is pending to be adjudged as insolvent
⚠️ If partners are reduced below 2 & LLP carries business with reduced partners for > 6 months, such partner [having knowledge that LLP is carrying business with him alone] becomes personally liable for obligations of LLP after such 6 months.
🏷️ §7 to 10 — Designated Partners (DP) §7-10 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
LLP shall have atleast 2 individual partners as DP [atleast 1 of them shall be resident in India].
📌 Resident in India means person who stayed in India for not less than 120 days during FY.
📌 If all partners are body corporate or < 2 individuals are partners, nominee of body corporate shall become DP [i.e. Firstly individuals are appointed and then, remaining DPs shall be nominees].
📌 In case of default, LLP and every partner is liable to penalty of ₹10,000 and further penalty of ₹100 per day [subject to maximum ₹1 lakh for LLP & ₹50,000 for partner].
DPIN — Designated Partner Identification Number:
  • DP shall obtain a Designated Partner Identification Number (DPIN) from CG
  • Section 153 to 159 of Companies Act shall apply mutatis mutandis
Consent to Act as DP:
  • Individual shall give prior consent to LLP to act as DP
  • LLP shall file particulars of DP with ROC within 30 days of his appointment
  • Default: LLP and every DP is liable to penalty of ₹5,000 and further penalty of ₹100 per day [subject to maximum ₹50,000 for LLP & ₹25,000 for every DP]
⚡ §8 — Liability of Designated Partners
Responsible for acts required to be done by LLP for compliance with act & LLP agreement
Liable to penalty imposed on LLP for contravention
🔄 §9 — Changes in Designated Partners
LLP may appoint DP within 30 days of vacancy & file particulars of new DP with ROC within 30 days.
If DP is not appointed (or if there is only 1 DP) → all partners are deemed as DP.
Default penalty: LLP and every partner is liable to penalty of ₹10,000 and further penalty of ₹100 per day [subject to maximum ₹1 lakh for LLP & ₹50,000 for partner].
🧠 DP Key Numbers
Min 2 DPs | Min 1 Resident (120 days in India) | File ROC in 30 days | Vacancy: fill in 30 days

  • Name of LLP, proposed business, address of registered office, name & address of partner & DP on incorporation and other prescribed information

    • Advocate/CA/CS/CMA engaged in formation of LLP, and
    • Anyone who subscribed to incorporation document
  • Note: If person makes statement which he knows to be false → punishable with imprisonment upto 2 years and fine not less than ₹10,000 extendable upto ₹5 Lakhs

🏷️ §15 to §17 — Name of LLP §15-17 ⭐ IMPORTANT
Reservation of Name:
  • A person shall apply to ROC in e-Form RUNLLP for reservation of name of proposed LLP or name to which LLP proposes to change its name
  • Note: LLP shall have words "limited liability partnership" or "LLP" as last words in name
  • Upon receipt of application, ROC may reserve name for 3 months from intimation by ROC
LLP shall NOT be registered with a name which in opinion of CG is:
  • Undesirable, OR
  • Identical or too nearly resemble to other LLP, company or registered trademark of another person
If LLP is registered with name which is identical or too nearly resembles to other LLP, company or registered trade mark, then CG (on application by LLP, company or proprietor of trademark within 3 years of incorporation, registration or change of name of LLP) may direct LLP to change its name within 3 months of issue of direction.
Change of Name:
If LLP changes its name, it shall give notice of change (along with order of CG) to ROC within 15 days of change. ROC shall make changes in certificate of incorporation and within 30 days of such change in certificate, LLP shall change its name in LLP agreement.
📌 Example
M/s Namo LLP incorporated on 1st May. A partner from M/s Namo (partnership firm) objected to LLP's name. Change not required as act applies if name resembles to company, LLP or trademark (& not firm).

🤝 §23 — Relationship of Partners §23
Mutual rights & duties of partners and LLP & its partners are governed by LLP agreement.
Note: In absence of agreement, mutual rights & duties are determined by First Schedule.
  • LLP agreement and any changes in it shall be filed with ROC
  • Agreement made before incorporation between persons who subscribe their names to incorporation document — may impose obligation on LLP (if ratified by all partners after incorporation)
📋 §24 — Partner's Transferable Interest & §42 — Transfer of Right
Transfer of Right of Partner (§42):
  • Right of a partner to profit & loss of LLP and to receive distributions as per LLP agreement is transferable (wholly or partly) to other person
  • Transfer of such right does NOT:
    • Entitle transferee to participate in management of LLP or access information of LLP
    • Cause disassociation of partner or dissolution & winding up of LLP
Cessation of Partnership Interest (§24):
  • If a person ceases to be partner, such former partner or person entitled to his share on his death/insolvency is entitled to receive:
    • Amount equal to capital contribution made to LLP by former partner, AND
    • His share in accumulated profits of LLP (after deduction of accumulated losses) as on date when such person ceases to be partner
  • Former partner or person entitled to his share on death/insolvency does NOT have right to interfere in management of LLP
📋 §25 — Registration of Changes in Particulars of Partners
Partner shall inform change in his name or address to LLP within 15 days of change.
Note: If case of default, partner shall be liable to penalty of ₹10,000.
LLP shall file notice with ROC:
  • Person becomes or ceases as partner: Within 30 days from becoming or ceasing as partner
  • Change in name or address of partner: Within 30 days of change (if partner intimates such change to LLP)
Default: LLP and every DP shall be liable to penalty of ₹10,000.
Notice filed with ROC shall be: Signed by DP and authenticated in prescribed manner; In prescribed form along with prescribed fees; If it relates to incoming partner → it shall contain statement by such partner (signed by him & authenticated in prescribed manner) that he consents to become partner.
📌 Person ceasing as partner may himself file notice with ROC, if he believes that LLP may not file notice and where notice is filed by partner, ROC shall obtain confirmation from LLP (except if LLP has also filed notice). If no confirmation is given by LLP within 15 days, ROC shall register notice made by ceasing partner.
⚖️ §26 to §30 — Extent & Limitation of Liability §26-30 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
Extent of Liability of LLP (§26 to §28):
  • Every partner of LLP is agent of LLP but NOT of other partners
  • LLP is liable if partner becomes liable due to wrongful act or omission in the course of business of LLP (or with authority)
  • LLP is NOT liable for anything done by partner if:
    • Partner has no authority to act for LLP, AND
    • Person knew that he has no authority (or does not believe that he is partner of LLP)
  • Liability of LLP shall be met out of property of LLP
Extent of Liability of Partner (§26-28):
  • Partner shall be personally liable for his own wrongful act or omission but NOT for wrongful act or omission of other partner of LLP
  • Generally, liability of partners is limited to their agreed contribution
🎭 §29 — Holding Out
Person [who by words or conduct represents or knowingly permits to be represented as partner of LLP] is liable to person who gave credit to LLP on faith of such representation (whether or not person know that representation has reached to person giving credit).
Note: If LLP is continued in same name after partner's death, use of such name or deceased partner name do not make his legal representative or estate liable for acts of LLP done after his death.
🚨 §30 — Liability in Case of Fraud & Compensation
Liability in Case of Fraud:
  • If an act is carried by LLP or partners for fraudulent purpose or to defraud creditor or any person:
  • Liability of LLP and such partners shall be unlimited for all debts/liabilities of LLP
  • Person who are knowingly a party to carry such fraudulent business → punishable with imprisonment upto 5 years and fine not less than ₹50,000 but extendable upto ₹5 Lakhs
  • Note: LLP is NOT liable if such act was done by partner without knowledge or authority of LLP
Compensation for Fraud (§30):
  • If LLP, partner, DP or employee of LLP has conducted affairs of LLP in fraudulent manner → it shall be liable to pay compensation to person who has suffered loss/damage due to such conduct
  • Note: LLP is NOT liable if partner, DP or employee acted fraudulently without knowledge of LLP
🧠 Liability Summary
LLP liability = Property of LLP | Partner liability = Own acts only (not others) | Fraud = UNLIMITED liability
💰 §32 & §33 — Contributions by Partners §32 & §33
Form of Contribution (§32):
  • Contribution of partner may consist of tangible, intangible, movable or immovable property or other benefit to LLP and contracts for services to be performed
  • Monetary value of contribution shall be disclosed in accounts of LLP
Obligation to Contribute (§33):
  • Obligation of partner to contribute to LLP shall be as per LLP agreement
  • Creditor (who acted as per obligation in LLP agreement) may enforce obligation of partner in original LLP agreement (if compromise made between partners without notice of creditor)
📊 §34 & §35 — Financial Disclosures §34 & §35 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
Proper Books of Accounts (BOA):
  • LLP shall maintain BOA at registered office on cash or accrual basis as per double entry system of accounting
  • Such accounts shall be audited [CG may exempt LLP]
Punishment for Default:
LLP: Not less than ₹25,000 but extendable upto ₹5 lakhs.
Every DP: Not less than ₹10,000 but extendable upto ₹1 lakh.
Statement of Account and Solvency:
  • LLP shall prepare Statement of account & solvency (as at last day of FY) within 6 months of end of FY
  • It shall be signed by DP
  • Default: LLP: Not less than ₹25,000 but extendable upto ₹5 lakhs. Every DP: Not less than ₹10,000 but extendable upto ₹1 lakh.
Annual Return:
  • LLP shall file duly authenticated annual return with ROC within 60 days of closure of FY
  • LLP shall file Statement of account & solvency with ROC every year
📌 In case of default in filing Statement of account & solvency or Annual Return with ROC:
LLP: ₹100 per day [Maximum ₹1 lakh].
Every DP: ₹100 per day [Maximum ₹50,000].
📌 Financial year (FY) means period from 1st April to 31st March of following year. However, FY for LLP incorporated after 30th September may end on 31st March of year next to following year.
🧠 Financial Disclosures Key Dates
Statement of Account: Within 6 months of end of FY | Annual Return: Within 60 days of closure of FY
📋 Other Important Sections — §31, §36, §37, §38, §39
Court or NCLT may reduce or waive penalty against any partner or employee of LLP if:
  • He has provided useful information during investigation of LLP, OR
  • Information given by him leads to LLP, partner or employee being convicted in any act

§36 Inspection: Incorporation Document, Name of partners & changes, Statement of account & solvency and Annual Return filed by LLP with ROC shall be available for inspection by any person in prescribed manner.
§38 Power of Registrar to Obtain Information:
  • ROC may require any person (including former partner) to answer question, make declaration or supply details/particulars as required by him within reasonable period
  • If person does not reply or ROC is not satisfied with reply → ROC can summon such person to appear before him, inspector or any public officer whom ROC designates

§37 Penalty for False Statement:
A person is punishable with imprisonment upto 2 years and fine not less than ₹1 lakh but extendable upto ₹5 lakhs, if he makes a statement in any document required under this act which is:
  • False in material particulars (which he knows to be false), OR
  • Omits words not defined in this act
§39 Compounding of Offences:
  • Application for compounding shall be made to ROC who shall forward it (along with his comments) to Regional Director or officer not below rank of Regional Director authorised by CG
  • Regional Director may compound any offence punishable with fine only by collecting not lower than minimum but extendable upto maximum fine
  • Offence committed by LLP, partner or DP within 3 years from compounding of its similar offence cannot be compounded
  • Intimation to ROC within 7 days of compounding

🤝 §60 to §62 — Compromise, Arrangement or Reconstruction §60-62
On application made by LLP, creditor, partner or liquidator for compromise or arrangement proposed between LLP & its creditors or LLP & its partners, NCLT may order meeting of creditors/partners to be called, held & conducted.
📌 NCLT (after application made to it) may stay commencement or continuation of suit or proceeding against LLP until such application is disposed off.
If majority in number representing 3/4th value of creditors or partners present & voting at meeting agree to compromise or arrangement AND if sanctioned by NCLT → it becomes binding on all creditors, partners, LLP, liquidator and contributories of LLP.
  • NCLT shall sanction compromise or arrangement only if all material facts of LLP (including latest financial position & pending investigation proceeding) is disclosed to it
  • NCLT order for sanction shall be filed by LLP with ROC within 30 days of making such order
  • If NCLT sanctions compromise or an arrangement of LLP, it may: Supervise carrying out of compromise or arrangement; Give directions or make modifications for proper working of compromise or arrangement
  • If NCLT is satisfied that compromise or arrangement sanctioned cannot be worked → may make order for winding up of LLP
  • Default: LLP and every DP is liable to penalty of ₹10,000 and further penalty of ₹100 per day after first during which default continues [subject to maximum ₹1 lakh for LLP & ₹50,000 for DP]
§62 — Provisions for Reconstruction/Amalgamation:
  • NCLT order for reconstruction or amalgamation — Transfer of undertaking, property or liabilities of transferor LLP to transferee LLP
  • Continuation of legal proceedings pending by/against transferor LLP for transferee LLP
  • Dissolution of transferor LLP (without winding up)
  • LLP shall NOT be amalgamated with a company
  • Filing with ROC: Within 30 days of NCLT order
🔚 §63 to §65 — Winding Up and Dissolution §63-65 ⭐ IMPORTANT
Winding up of LLP may either be voluntary or by NCLT and such LLP may be dissolved.
LLP may be wound up by NCLT if:
LLP decides to be wound up by NCLT
Number of partners reduced below 2 for > 6 months
LLP acted against sovereignty & integrity of India, security of state or public order
LLP made default in filing Statement of account & solvency or Annual Return with ROC for 5 consecutive FYs
NCLT is of opinion that it is just & equitable
📌 CG may make rules for winding up and dissolution of LLP.

Q1 · 4M · §7-10 XYZ LLP has 3 body corporate partners and no individual partners. State the rules regarding Designated Partners in such a scenario. What is the consequence of default in appointing DP?

LLP shall file particulars of DP with ROC within 30 days of appointment.

Q2 · 4M · §26-30 ABC LLP has partners P, Q and R. Partner P entered into a contract with XYZ Ltd. without authority of LLP. XYZ Ltd. was aware that P had no authority. Is LLP liable to XYZ Ltd.? Is P personally liable?
PROVISION§26 to §28: LLP is NOT liable for anything done by partner if partner has no authority to act for LLP AND person knew that he has no authority. Partner is personally liable for his own wrongful act or omission but NOT for wrongful act of other partners.
APPLICATIONP entered into contract without authority. XYZ Ltd. was aware that P had no authority. Therefore, two conditions are satisfied: (1) P had no authority, (2) XYZ Ltd. knew about it. Hence LLP is NOT liable to XYZ Ltd. However, P being the wrongdoer is personally liable for his own wrongful act or omission.
CONCLUSIONLLP is NOT liable to XYZ Ltd. as XYZ Ltd. knew P had no authority. P is personally liable for his own wrongful act. Liability of LLP shall be met out of property of LLP only.

Q5 · 4M · §30 LLP carried out fraudulent business activities with the knowledge of partners P and Q (but not partner R). State the liability of LLP, P, Q and R.

LLP is NOT liable if act was done by partner without knowledge or authority of LLP.

👥 §103 — Quorum for Meetings §103 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
Quorum = Minimum number of members to be personally present to constitute a valid meeting.
📌 Quorum shall be present both at commencement of meeting & also while transacting business. If quorum is not present while transacting a business, it cannot be transacted & is a nullity.
Public Company:
  • If number of members ≤ 1000: 5 members personally present
  • If members are > 1000 but ≤ 5000: 15 members personally present
  • If number of members > 5000: 30 members personally present
Private Company:
2 members personally present
  • AOA may provide for a larger number for quorum
  • Personally present means: Members entitled to vote in meeting [i.e., Proxy shall NOT be included but authorised representatives of company & President of India or Governor of State shall be included for calculation of quorum]
  • If 2 or more companies appoint a single person as representative → then each company is counted for quorum (i.e., such single person shall be counted multiple times for quorum)
  • Member (not entitled to vote due to it being related party) shall be counted for quorum
📌 Example
D Ltd. has 965 members. Its AOA requires 7 members to be personally present for quorum. Following attended the meeting: A (Governor's representative), B & C (preference shareholders), D (representing Y Ltd. & Z Ltd.), and E, F & H (proxies). Is quorum present?

Solution: Only members entitled to vote and personally present is counted for quorum. A counts; D counts as 2 (for Y Ltd. & Z Ltd.); B & C are excluded as preference shareholders (not entitled to vote); Proxy E, F & H are NOT counted. Total = 3. Quorum of 7 is not met and Meeting is invalid.
Adjournment of Meeting for want of Quorum:
If quorum is not present within half an hour from time appointed for meeting:
  • If meeting called by BOD → Meeting shall be adjourned to same day, time & place in the next week (or such date, time & place as BOD determine). Company shall give not less than 3 days' notice (either individually or by advertisement in 1 english & 1 vernacular language newspaper in circulation at place where registered office of company is situated)
  • If meeting called by requisitionists → it shall stand cancelled [i.e., EGM called by requisitionists]
If quorum is not present in adjourned meeting within half an hour → members present in such meeting shall form quorum.
Note: If meeting is adjourned for other reason (such as behaviour of members) → original quorum is required in adjourned meeting.
📌 If a resolution is passed at adjourned meeting of members, resolution is treated as passed on adjourned meeting date (and not original meeting date). [§116]

≤1000 members = 5 | 1001-5000 = 15 | >5000 = 30 | Private = 2

📜 §114 — Types of Resolutions §114 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
A motion is a proposal which becomes resolution if it obtains necessary votes [i.e., Where a proposal (motion) is put for consideration at meeting, if it gets passed, it is considered as resolution].
Ordinary Resolution (OR):
  • Notice shall be duly given
  • It is passed if votes cast in favour [including casting vote of chairman] exceed votes cast against resolution
Special Resolution (SR):

  • Notice shall be duly given

Q3 · 4M · §105 Mr. A holds 15% of share capital of XYZ Ltd. and wants to appoint Mr. B as his proxy for 5 companies where Mr. A is a member. Is this valid? What is the maximum number of members a proxy can represent?
PROVISION§105: A person can act as proxy for ≤ 50 members holding (in aggregate) not more than 10% of share capital (carrying voting rights). Member holding > 10% of share capital may appoint a single person as proxy and such person shall not act as proxy for any other person.
APPLICATIONMr. A holds 15% of share capital → MORE than 10% of share capital. Therefore, Mr. A may appoint a single person (Mr. B) as proxy. However, since Mr. A holds > 10%, Mr. B cannot act as proxy for any other person. Hence, Mr. B cannot act as proxy for other 4 companies where Mr. A is a member (if those are for the same meeting of XYZ Ltd.).
CONCLUSIONMr. A can appoint Mr. B as proxy for XYZ Ltd. meeting. But since Mr. A holds >10%, Mr. B cannot act as proxy for any other person for that meeting. Normal proxy limit: max 50 members holding in aggregate ≤ 10% of share capital.
