👤 §141 — Qualification of Auditor §141(1) ⭐ IMPORTANT
Individual: Chartered Accountant (CA) holding a valid Certificate of Practice
Firm / LLP: Majority of partners must be practicing CA in India
Only CA partners can sign on behalf of firm
🚫 §141(3) — Disqualifications §141(3) ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
🧠 Master Mnemonic
" C A S H A ko audit nahi karne do! "
C = Body Corporate (LLP excluded from this disq.)
A = Officer / Appointed employee of company (or partner/employee of officer/employee)
S = Security/Interest holder — relative or partner holds security/interest in company, Assoc., Subsidiary, Holding, or Any subsidiary of Holding (CASHA group) with face value > ₹1 lakh
H = Has indebtedness > ₹5 lakhs to CASHA group
A = Audit in more than 20 companies (partner/individual limit)
  • All relatives together may hold security of face value not exceeding ₹1 lakh in such companies
  • If relative acquires security exceeding ₹1 lakh after appointment → auditor must take corrective action within 60 days
  • Guarantor for indebtedness of third person in excess of ₹1 lakh to CASHA → disqualified
  • Business relationship with CASHA = transaction for commercial purpose (except: professional services transactions & transactions in ordinary course at arm's length price)
  • Partner/partner of firm who is auditor in more than 20 companies as on appointment date
RuleDetail
Individual limitMax 20 companies
Firm limitNo. of CA partners × 20
NOT counted in 20 limitPrivate company (paid-up <₹100 cr, not defaulted u/s 92 & 137), Dormant company, OPC, Small company
⚠️ Private co limit: Appointment in private company having paid-up share capital <₹100 crores (not defaulted u/s 92 & 137), dormant company, OPC and small company — NOT be considered within limit of 20 companies.
In case of firm, limit of 20 audits is per person but if a person is partner in multiple firms, all such firms in which he is a partner will together be entitled to 20 audits on his account.
Vacation of Office: If auditor incurs any disqualification AFTER appointment → shall vacate office → treated as casual vacancy
🔢 §141 — Solved Example: 20 Company Limit
Example: ABC & Co. has Mr. A, B and C as partners who are auditors in 4, 6 and 10 companies respectively. What is maximum number of audits?
Solution:
Total firm limit = 3 × 20 = 60
Audits already held = 4 + 6 + 10 = 20
ABC & Co. can accept 40 more audits
Partner A: 20−4 = 16 more | Partner B: 20−6 = 14 more | Partner C: 20−10 = 10 more
🏛️ §139 — Appointment of Auditors §139 ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
🏛️ Government Company
First Auditor
CAG within 60 days of registration
CAG fails?
BOD appoints within next 30 days
BOD fails?
Members inform → appoint at EGM (within 60 days)
Subsequent Auditor
CAG appoints within 180 days of FY commencement
Casual Vacancy
CAG fills within 30 days. CAG fails → BOD in 30 days
🏢 Other Company
First Auditor
BOD within 30 days of registration
BOD fails?
Inform members → Members appoint at EGM
Subsequent Auditor
BOD recommends name → Members appoint at AGM. Hold office till 6th AGM
Casual Vacancy
BOD fills within 30 days. Hold office till next AGM
🧠 Timeline Trick
GOV = 60 days | OTHERS = 30 days | CASUAL = 30 days (both)
📌 First Auditor Note: holds office until conclusion of 1st AGM. Period as first auditor is NOT to be considered for calculating term as subsequent auditor.
📅 Term & Cooling Period §139(2)
Company TypeIndividualFirm / LLP
Listed / Unlisted public (paid-up ≥₹10cr) / Private (paid-up ≥₹50cr) / Public borrowings/deposits ≥₹50cr 1 term of 5 years 2 terms of 5 years each
Other companiesAny number of terms
⏳ Cooling Period = 5 years — after completing term, NOT eligible for re-appointment in SAME company.
Key: Cooling period does NOT apply to subsidiary, holding, or any other company — ONLY same company!
  • On date of appointment of audit firm: shall NOT have any common partner with outgoing firm whose tenure expired in immediately preceding FY. If common partners exist → cooling period of 5 years.
  • If a partner (who certifies FS) retires from audit firm (which has completed term) and joins another firm → such other firm also becomes ineligible for 5 years
  • A person shall NOT be eligible to be appointed as auditor if it is associated with outgoing auditor under same network (same brand name, trade name or common control)
  • Cooling off period for a company does NOT apply to any other company (example: such auditor can audit company's subsidiary or any service except statutory audit)
📣 §139 — Rotation of Auditor
Members of company may provide that:
• Audit conducted by more than 1 auditor, OR
• In case of firm: audit partner & his team shall be rotated at intervals decided by members
If company appointed joint auditors → company may follow rotation in manner that all joint auditors do not complete their term in same year
📌 §177 — Audit Committee: Companies Required to Constitute
Must Constitute Audit Committee:
  • Listed public company
  • Unlisted public co having (on date of last audited FS):
  • Paid up capital ≥ ₹10 crores, OR
  • Turnover ≥ ₹100 crores, OR
  • Aggregate outstanding loan, borrowings, debenture & deposits ≥ ₹50 crores
Exception: Company is exempt from constituting committee if it was earlier required to constitute but ceases to fulfil above conditions for 3 consecutive years
If Audit Committee required:
  1. Audit Committee recommends auditor name to BOD
  2. If BOD agrees → recommend to members at AGM
  3. If BOD disagrees → refer back to Audit Committee with reasons
  4. If AC still does not reconsider → BOD shall record reasons & send its own recommendation to AGM
If Audit Committee NOT required:
  1. BOD itself recommends name to members
  2. Members appoint at AGM
📌 Before appointment: written consent + certificate (eligible, not disqualified, as per term, within limits, no proceedings against) must be obtained

🚫 §144 — Services Auditor CANNOT Provide §144 ⭐ IMPORTANT
Rule: Auditor shall provide services approved by BOD or audit committee ONLY. But shall NOT include following services (directly or indirectly) to company, its holding or subsidiary company.
🧠 Mnemonic — Remember All 9
" I A D M · I O I A O "
Investment banking services  |  Actuarial services  |  Design & implementation of FIS  |  Management services
Investment advisory services  |  Outsourced financial services (rendering)  |  Internal audit  |  Accounting & bookkeeping  |  Other prescribed services
#Prohibited ServiceWhy Important
(i)Investment banking servicesConflict of interest
(ii)Actuarial servicesSelf-review threat
(iii)Design & implementation of financial information systemSelf-review threat
(iv)Management servicesManagement threat
(v)Investment advisory servicesConflict of interest
(vi)Rendering of outsourced financial servicesSelf-review threat
(vii)Internal auditSelf-review threat
(viii)Accounting and book keeping servicesSelf-review threat
(ix)Other prescribed servicesAs notified
⚠️ Note on "directly or indirectly":
• If auditor is individual: Himself or through his relative or person connected with him
• If auditor is firm: Itself or through its partners or its parent, subsidiary or associate entity or other entity in which firm or its partner has significant influence, or whose name, trademark or brand is used by firm or its partners
🔍 §143(12) — Reporting of Frauds §143(12) ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
🚨 Yeh topic ICAI exams mein bahut zyada aata hai! Time limits exactly yaad rakho!
Fraud < ₹1 Crore
By officers/employees of company
  1. Report to Audit Committee or BOD within 2 days
  2. Specify: Nature, Approximate Amount, Parties involved
  3. AC/BOD discloses details in BOD Report
Fraud ≥ ₹1 Crore
By officers/employees of company
  1. Report to Audit Committee/BOD within 2 days
  2. Seek reply/observation within 45 days
  3. Reply received → Forward to CG within 15 days of receipt
  4. No reply in 45 days → Forward to CG directly (with note)
📬 How to Send Report to CG:
Secretary, MCA in sealed cover by Registered Post with Acknowledgement Due or Speed Post followed by e-mail in confirmation of same
Report shall be on letter head of auditor containing his postal address, e-mail, contact number & be signed by auditor with his seal & indicate his membership number
Non-compliance PenaltyAmount
Listed company₹5 lakhs
Any other company₹1 lakh
📌 Note: Auditor shall report to CG only if he is first person to identify fraud during performance of his duties. But in all cases, he shall still report to BOD/Audit Committee.
📋 §143 — Branch Audit §143
Branch in India:
Accounts audited by company's auditor OR person qualified for appointment as auditor of company
Branch outside India:
Accounts audited by company's auditor OR an accountant OR person qualified to be auditor as per laws of that country
📌 Branch auditor shall submit his report to company's auditor
📄 §143 & §145 — Auditor's Report
Auditor shall express opinion & make report to members on accounts & FS examined by him — as to whether FS give a true and fair view of state of affairs of company at end of FY and other prescribed matters.
  • Whether he has all obtained information & explanation necessary for audit
  • Whether proper BOA have been kept & proper returns adequate for audit been received
  • Whether report on accounts of branch office audited by others has been dealt with
  • Whether company's balance sheet and P&L account are in agreement with BOA and returns
  • Whether FS comply with Accounting Standards
  • Qualification, reservation or adverse remark on maintenance of accounts
  • Observation/comment on financial transactions having adverse effect on functioning of company
  • Whether any director is disqualified from being appointed u/s 164(2)
  • Whether company has adequate internal financial controls (not applicable to small private companies)
  • Such other matters as may be prescribed (CARO 2020)
If any above matter is answered in negative or with qualification → auditor's report shall state reason for same
  • Auditor shall sign auditor's report or sign/certify other documents of company
  • Qualifications, observations or comments on financial transactions or matters having adverse effect on functioning of company → shall be read at general meeting & be open to inspection by any member of company
  • CARO 2020 should be complied by statutory auditor of every company on which it applies
  • Every auditor shall comply with auditing standards
🏛️ Audit of Government Companies
In case of government company → CAG shall direct the auditor the manner in which accounts of company shall be audited. Then auditor shall submit copy of audit report to CAG which shall include directions issued by CAG, action taken and its impact on accounts & FS.
Within 60 days of receipt of audit report, CAG has right to:
  • Conduct supplementary audit of FS by person authorized by it
  • Comment on or supplement to audit report
  • Laid before AGM at same time as audit report
If considered necessary:
CAG may order test audit of accounts of company

🔎 §143(1) — Duties of Inquiry
🧠 Mnemonic — LAST PAB
L · A · S · T · P · A · B
Loans & Advances given by company — properly secured? Terms not prejudicial to company or members?
Advances given as deposits — shown as deposits?

📢 §146 — Auditor to Attend General Meeting
All notice & communication for general meeting shall be forwarded to auditor
Auditor shall mandatorily attend GM of company either by himself or through his authorized representative (who shall also be qualified to be auditor)
Auditor shall have right to be heard at such meetings on any part of business which concerns him as auditor
🔄 §140 — Removal of Auditor Before Term §140(1) ⭐ HIGH PRIORITY
Principle: Auditor can be removed from office before expiry of his term after giving him a reasonable opportunity of being heard (Audi Alteram Partem)
  1. Pass Board Resolution (BR) — after giving auditor reasonable opportunity of being heard
  2. Make application in Form ADT-2 to Central Government within 30 days of passing BR
  3. Obtain approval from CG
  4. Hold General Meeting within 60 days of CG approval and pass Special Resolution
🧠 Removal Steps
BR → ADT-2 to CG (30 days) → CG Approval → SR in GM (60 days)
📤 §140(3) — Resignation by Auditor
If auditor has resigned from company → file statement in Form ADT-3 indicating reasons & other facts to:
• Company
• ROC within 30 days of resignation
Government Company: Also file statement with CAG
Default Penalty:
Fine: ₹50,000 or amount equal to remuneration of auditor (whichever is less)
+ ₹500 per day during which failure continues
Subject to maximum of ₹2 lakhs
🔁 §139(9)&(10) — Re-appointment of Retiring Auditor
Retiring auditor may be re-appointed at AGM if:
  • He is not disqualified for re-appointment
  • He has not given notice in writing of his unwillingness to be re-appointed
  • SR is not passed to appoint other auditor or provide he shall not be re-appointed
If no auditor appointed at AGM: existing auditor shall continue as auditor of company
📢 Special Notice — Appointing Other than Retiring Auditor
When required? When company wants to appoint someone OTHER THAN retiring auditor or provide that retiring auditor shall NOT be re-appointed
RuleDetail
Who can give?Members holding ≥1% voting power OR shares on which ≥₹5 lakhs is paid up
TimingNot earlier than 3 months but at least 14 days before meeting
On receipt of noticeCompany shall send copy of notice to retiring auditor
If retiring auditor makes representationCompany shall: State fact in notice sent to members, Send copy of representation to members
If representation not sentMay be read at meeting & copy filed with ROC
⚠️ If NCLT satisfied that rights are being abused by retiring auditor → representation need NOT be sent & need NOT be read out at meeting
🔓 Removal of Auditor who has Acted Fraudulently (NCLT)
If NCLT is satisfied (suo moto or on application by CG or any concerned person) that auditor has acted in fraudulent manner OR abetted/colluded in fraud → may direct company to change its auditors
NCLT shall order within 15 days of receipt of application
Auditor against whom final order is passed → NOT appointed as auditor of any company for 5 years & also liable u/s 447

🏛️ NFRA — National Financial Reporting Authority New Topic
Actions for Auditor's Oversight
Review
Review working paper (including audit plan & other documents) and communication related to audit
Evaluate
Evaluate sufficiency of quality control system and manner of documentation by auditor
Perform
Perform other testing of audit, supervisory & quality control procedures of auditor as necessary
  • Auditor to report on governance practices & internal processes to promote audit quality, protect its reputation & reduce risk (like failure of auditor) & take necessary action on report
  • Additional information or personal presence of auditor for additional information or explanation
  • NFRA shall NOT publish proprietary/confidential information (except if it has reasons to do so in public interest & records reasons in writing)
  • Sending separate report containing proprietary/confidential information to CG
  • If NFRA believes that any law, professional standard or standard is violated by auditor → it may decide on further course of investigation or enforcement action through its concerned division
Perform its monitoring & enforcement activities through its officers or experts with sufficient experience in audit of relevant industry
Publish its findings of non-compliances on its website and in manner as it considers fit (except if it has reasons not to do so in public interest & records reasons in writing)
  • NFRA may direct auditor to take measures for improvement of audit quality including changes in audit processes, quality control & audit reports & specify a detailed plan with time-limits
  • It is duty of auditor to make required improvements and send report to NFRA explaining how it has complied with directions
  • NFRA shall monitor improvements made by auditor & take action as it deems fit
  • NFRA may refer cases of overseeing quality of service of auditor to Quality Review Board
  • NFRA may take assistance of experts for oversight & monitoring activities
📅 Important: Auditor of company governed by NFRA → shall file return with NFRA on or before 30th November every year in Form NFRA-2

Q1 · 4M · §141 ABC & Co. has 3 CA partners — A (auditor in 4 cos), B (6 cos), C (10 cos). What is max number of audits ABC & Co. and each partner can still accept?
PROVISIONSection 141(3): CA firm cannot be auditor in more than 20 companies. Firm limit = No. of CA partners × 20.
APPLICATION
Total firm limit = 3 × 20 = 60. Already held = 4+6+10 = 20. Remaining = 40 more.
Partner A: 20−4=16 more | Partner B: 20−6=14 more | Partner C: 20−10=10 more
CONCLUSIONABC & Co. can accept 40 more audits. Individual: A=16, B=14, C=10.
Q2 · 4M · §139 M Ltd. (listed company) appointed X as first auditor (held till 1st AGM). Then re-appointed till 6th AGM. BOD now wants to re-appoint X again. Valid?
PROVISIONSection 139(2): Listed company's individual auditor = 1 term of 5 years. Period as first auditor is NOT counted for subsequent term.
APPLICATIONFirst auditor period (till 1st AGM) — excluded. Subsequent: 1st AGM to 6th AGM = 5 years = 1 completed term. X has completed his allowed 1 term → NOT eligible for re-appointment.
CONCLUSIONNOT valid. X must observe 5-year cooling period before re-appointment in M Ltd.
Q3 · 5M · §143 Statutory auditor discovers fraud of ₹2 crores committed by CFO of PQR Ltd. Audit Committee takes 60 days to respond. What should auditor do? All time limits.
PROVISIONSection 143(12): Fraud ≥ ₹1 Cr by officers/employees → report to AC/BOD within 2 days → seek reply in 45 days → if reply, forward to CG in 15 days → if no reply, forward to CG directly.
APPLICATION
₹2 Cr ≥ ₹1 Cr → Step 1: Report to AC/BOD within 2 days. Step 2: Seek reply within 45 days. AC took 60 days = NO reply in 45 days → Step 3: Forward report to CG directly with note that no reply received. Sent via sealed cover, Registered Post + e-mail.
CONCLUSIONAuditor must forward report directly to CG since AC did not reply within 45 days. Non-compliance penalty: ₹5 lakhs (listed company).
Q4 · 4M · §141 Are these persons disqualified? (a) Mr. A — CA, owes ₹6 lakhs to company. (b) XYZ Firm — provides management consulting. (c) Mr. B — relative holds ₹80,000 face value shares. (d) Mr. C — full-time employee elsewhere.
PROVISIONSection 141(3) — Disqualifications: indebtedness >₹5L, providing prohibited services u/s 144, security >₹1L face value, full-time employment elsewhere.
APPLICATION
(a) DISQUALIFIED — ₹6L > ₹5L threshold [§141(3)(d)]
(b) DISQUALIFIED — Management services prohibited u/s 144 [renders auditor disqualified]
(c) NOT DISQUALIFIED — ₹80,000 face value < ₹1 lakh threshold [§141(3)(d)]
(d) DISQUALIFIED — Full-time employment elsewhere [§141(3)(b)]
CONCLUSIONA, B, D — Disqualified. Only Mr. B — NOT disqualified (₹80K < ₹1L threshold).
Q5 · 4M · §140 Board of Directors of Moon Ltd. wants to remove auditor Mr. Verma before expiry of his term. State complete procedure with relevant sections.
PROVISIONSection 140(1): Auditor can be removed before term expiry after giving reasonable opportunity of being heard (Audi Alteram Partem) by following prescribed procedure.
APPLICATION
  1. Pass Board Resolution — give Mr. Verma opportunity to be heard
  2. File Form ADT-2 to CG within 30 days of BR
  3. Obtain CG approval
  4. Pass Special Resolution in GM within 60 days of CG approval
CONCLUSIONMoon Ltd. must follow all 4 steps. Direct removal without CG approval is NOT permitted u/s 140(1).
Q6 · 5M · §147 Mr. Sharma (auditor) wilfully gave incorrect information in audit report to benefit company promoters. What are the legal consequences u/s 147?
PROVISIONSection 147: If contravention is made knowingly or wilfully with intention to deceive company, shareholders, creditors or tax authorities → enhanced punishment applies.
APPLICATION
Since Mr. Sharma acted wilfully with intent to deceive:
• Imprisonment up to 1 year
• Fine: not less than ₹50,000 extendable to lower of ₹25 lakh or 8× remuneration
• Refund remuneration to company
• Pay damages to company, statutory body, members, creditors
CONCLUSIONMr. Sharma faces criminal prosecution including imprisonment up to 1 year, heavy fine, refund of remuneration, and liability to pay damages.
