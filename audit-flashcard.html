Ch 1 — Nature, Objective & Scope of Audit
Definition of Audit
ElementDetail
NatureIndependent examination of financial information
Applies toAny entity — profit/non-profit, any size/form
PurposeExpress an opinion on FS
ResponsibilityManagement prepares FS; Auditor examines
Objective of Audit (SA 200)
ObjectiveDetail
PrimaryObtain Reasonable Assurance — FS free from material misstatement
SecondaryReport on FS as per auditor's findings
MisstatementDue to Fraud OR Error or Both
Assurance levelHigh level (Reasonable) — NOT Absolute
Scope of Audit — Includes
#Coverage
1Coverage of all entity aspects relevant to FS
2Reliability & sufficiency of financial information
3Proper disclosure of financial information
Scope — Does NOT Include
ExcludedReason
Civil structure suitabilityNeeds engineer expertise
Document authenticationAuditor NOT an expert
Official investigationNo legal powers of search/oath
Outside competence dutiesNot expected

Memory Mnemonics
🧠 RAID = Reasonable Assurance | Independent | FS as a whole | Detect fraud/error 🧠 5 Elements of Assurance = 3P + SM + SC + SAE + WAR (3 Party, Subject Matter, Suitable Criteria, Sufficient Appropriate Evidence, Written Assurance Report) 🧠 Benefits of Audit = HIGH CREAM (High quality info, Interest safeguard, Government tax, Help lenders, Check fraud, Review controls, Employee moral check, Audit mandated) 🧠 Planning Benefits = DAP-DSC (Devote attention, Address problems, Proper organization, Direct team, Supervise work, Coordinate experts) 🧠 Audit vs Review = Audit=Reasonable (High) | Review=Limited (Moderate) | Both = Historical FI 🧠 Scope NOT include = I-ADE (Investigation powers, Authenticate documents, Determine civil structure life, External expertise needed)
