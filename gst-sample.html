What does GST stand for?

Goods and Services Tax — a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India, replacing multiple state and central taxes.

What are the GST tax slabs in India?

GST has four main slabs: 5%, 12%, 18%, and 28%. Essential items are at 0% or 5%, while luxury goods attract 28%. Some items also attract a cess over 28%.

What is the full form of GSTIN?

GSTIN stands for Goods and Services Tax Identification Number — a 15-digit unique number assigned to every registered taxpayer under GST.

What is the composition scheme under GST?

Small businesses with turnover up to ₹1.5 crore (₹75 lakh for some states) can opt for the composition scheme, paying a flat rate of tax and filing quarterly returns with simplified compliance.

What is the difference between zero-rated and exempt supply under GST?

Zero-rated supplies (exports, SEZ supplies) attract 0% GST but ITC is allowed. Exempt supplies (basic food, health services) have no GST and ITC is NOT allowed — making them distinct in tax treatment.
